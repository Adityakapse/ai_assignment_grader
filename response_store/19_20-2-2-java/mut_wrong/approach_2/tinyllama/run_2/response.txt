As per Reverse Polish Notation / Postfix Notation, what is the notation for computing O(lcm(a,b)) using GCD (lowercase "g" and "c")?

A: The notation is called postfix notation or stack notation because each operand is separated from each other by a operator. For example, 1 * 2 + 3 * 4 would be written as follows: 1 g (+) 2 * 3 4 * +.
To compute O(lcm(a,b)), we first push the lowercase "g" and "c", then take the uppermost two numbers on the stack (i.e., push the two numbers together), the GCD of the two operands, and push the result back onto the stack. After processing one iteration, the process is reversed: take the result from the top, push the GCD of the two operands from the previous iteration, push the original two numbers, then compute another iteration of O(lcm(a,b)). After processing several iterations, we have the final answer.
